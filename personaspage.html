Personas

PROBLEM: How can we better understand how users interact with interfaces through personas?

In this project, I explored how real users interacted with an interface and created personas based on my observations. I chose a toaster oven as my interface, as this is an easily accessible product that many people have in their homes. The process included the following steps:

  1. Preparing questions and interviewing individuals using a toaster oven
  2. Making objective observations on how users interacted with the toaster oven
  3. Creating personas based on these users
  4. Illustrating a storyboard for one of the created personas

Key features of the toaster oven that I observed individuals using were:


USER RESEARCH

Some questions I asked my interviewees included:

  1. When would you choose to use this toaster oven over another kitchen appliance, and for what purposes / types of cooking?
  2. How effective do you think the dials are on a toaster oven, and is it easy to understand and use?
  3. Which dial do you most frequently use and why?
  4. How effective is representing time and heat in the circular fashion of a dial?

I interviewed my mom, dad, and my friend because they represented a wide range of age, gender, and socioeconomic demographics. During these interviews, I noticed:

  • All users instinctively knew how to use the toaster oven -- they knew how to set the time and heat dials, showing that comprehending these functions is universal
  • Everyone focused more on the time dial instead of the heat dial, and adjusted the dials approximately instead of turning to exact values
  • Everyone enjoyed the practicality and simplicity of the toaster, but some wished for more complex functions

PERSONAS

After considering my observations, I made two personas represented different users interacting with the toaster oven. This helped give more context to how users interact with the toaster oven and pushed me to think about what problems users might encounter.




STORYBOARD

The storyboard helped me better visualize a user's journey from start to end. I chose to create a storyboard for Peggy, as I think her interaction and challenges with the toaster oven are very relatable.


KEY TAKEAWAYS

  • Crafting nuanced questions are really important in getting the best data possible from users.
  • Understanding how users interact with an interface is important, as users may have challenges and feedback that designers hadn't previously thought of.
  • As emotions are highly integrated into how individuals use interfaces, personas and storyboards can help inform designers on a users' journey in using the product.
  • Toaster ovens are a practical and easy-to-use product! :)
